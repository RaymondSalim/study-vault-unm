## Lecture Slides Overview

| Lecture | Topic | Key Concepts | Exam Weight |
|---------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| 1-2 | Boolean Logic and Gates | Boolean algebra, truth tables, AND/OR/NOT/NAND/NOR/XOR gates, De Morgan's laws, Karnaugh maps, Boolean simplification | 15% |
| 3-4 | Combinational Circuits | Half adders, full adders, ripple-carry adders, multiplexers, demultiplexers, decoders, encoders, ALU design | 15% |

## Notes

- Computer Architecture is the single heaviest topic (~20%) — understand the CPU thoroughly.
- Boolean/Gates and Circuits together form ~45% of the exam — expect significant calculation/drawing.
- Machine Code questions often ask you to trace through instructions showing register/memory state changes.
- OS and Networking are more theory-based — focus on understanding concepts rather than calculation.
- Karnaugh maps for up to 4 variables are commonly examined; practise finding minimal expressions.
