# Study Order

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Vision & Scope | Sets the project context; everything else flows from understanding the big picture | 2 hours |
| 2 | Requirements Types | Must understand functional vs non-functional before gathering them | 2 hours |
| 3 | Elicitation | Learn how to gather requirements from stakeholders using various techniques | 3 hours |
| 4 | Analysis & Modelling | Structure and refine the gathered requirements; depends on knowing what you collected | 3 hours |
| 5 | SRS Document | Formal documentation; requires understanding of all requirement types and models | 3 hours |
| 6 | Negotiation & Validation | Checking requirements are correct and resolving conflicts; needs completed SRS context | 2.5 hours |
| 7 | Requirements Management | Ongoing tracking and change control; makes most sense after understanding the full lifecycle | 2 hours |
| 8 | Requirements Testing | Verifying implementation meets requirements; logically the final step in the process | 2.5 hours |

## Dependency Rationale

The study order follows the natural requirements engineering lifecycle:

1. **Understand context** (Vision/Scope) before you can gather requirements
2. **Know what to look for** (Types) before eliciting
3. **Gather** (Elicitation) before you can organise
4. **Analyse** (Modelling) before you can document formally
5. **Document** (SRS) before you can validate
6. **Validate** (Negotiation) before you can manage changes
7. **Manage** (Management) throughout, but understood best after seeing the full picture
8. **Test** (Testing) as the final verification step
