## Recommended Study Sequence

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | SDLC and Processes | Provides the big-picture framework; everything else fits within this lifecycle | 3 hours |
| 2 | Requirements Engineering | First phase of any project; defines what to build before designing | 3 hours |
| 3 | Use Cases and Scenarios | Natural extension of requirements; bridges requirements to design | 3 hours |
| 4 | UML Class Diagrams | Core design skill; heavily tested and builds on requirements understanding | 4 hours |
| 5 | UML Sequence Diagrams | Shows dynamic behaviour of the classes you designed; complements class diagrams | 3 hours |
| 6 | OOP Design Principles | Evaluates quality of your designs; connects class diagrams to good practice | 4 hours |
| 7 | Testing | Final lifecycle phase; tests validate requirements are met and design works | 3 hours |

## Study Tips

- **Start with SDLC** to understand where each activity fits in the development process.
- **Requirements before Design** mirrors real-world practice — you need to know what to build before designing it.
- **UML is heavily examined** — practise drawing diagrams by hand, not just reading them.
- **Design Principles** make more sense after you have drawn several class diagrams and can see what good/bad design looks like.
- **Testing last** because it is the most self-contained topic and benefits from understanding the full lifecycle.

## Total Estimated Time: ~23 hours

Plan to spend extra time practising diagram drawing — this is a skill that improves with repetition.
