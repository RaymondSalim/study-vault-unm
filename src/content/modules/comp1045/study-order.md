# Study Order

## Recommended Learning Path

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Vectors & Matrices | Foundation for linear algebra; matrix operations used in transformations and systems | 4-5 hours |
| 2 | Linear Systems | Applies matrix skills to solve simultaneous equations (Gaussian elimination) | 3-4 hours |
| 3 | Determinants & Eigenvalues | Requires matrix knowledge; eigenvalues appear in ODEs and transformations | 4-5 hours |
| 4 | Linear Transformations | Combines matrices + determinants; geometric understanding of linear maps | 3-4 hours |
| 5 | Calculus | Differentiation and integration techniques; independent of linear algebra but needed for series and ODEs | 5-6 hours |
| 6 | Probability | Independent topic; defines random variables, expected value, variance | 4-5 hours |
| 7 | Distributions | Builds directly on probability; Binomial, Poisson, Normal | 4-5 hours |
| 8 | Graph Theory | Largely independent; can be studied any time but placed last as it is typically lowest exam weight | 3-4 hours |

## Rationale

Linear algebra topics (1-4) build sequentially and form a coherent block. Calculus (5) is largely independent but is needed before differential equations if they are covered. Probability and distributions (6-7) form their own chain. Graph theory (8) is standalone.
