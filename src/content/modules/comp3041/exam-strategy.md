### How to Structure an Ethical Analysis Answer

**Step 1**: Identify the ethical dilemma (what competing values are in tension?)

**Step 2**: Apply multiple ethical frameworks:
- **Consequentialist**: What are the outcomes? Who benefits? Who is harmed? What maximises net good?
- **Deontological**: What rules/duties apply? Would this universalise? Are people treated as means?
- **Virtue Ethics**: What would a virtuous professional do? What character traits are relevant?

**Step 3**: Apply the SE Code (if relevant):
- Identify most relevant principles
- Cite specific clause numbers
- Determine if action aligns with or contradicts clauses

**Step 4**: Reach a justified conclusion — acknowledge complexity, state your position clearly

### Common Mistakes in Ethical Analysis Essays

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Only applying one framework | Always use at least 2-3 frameworks |
| Not citing specific clause numbers | Reference "Clause 1.04" not just "the Code says..." |
| Stating opinion without justification | Every conclusion needs supporting reasoning |
| Ignoring counterarguments | Address the strongest opposing view |
| Confusing legal with ethical | State whether something is legal AND whether it's ethical (they can differ) |
| Being vague about stakeholders | Name specific affected parties |

### Night-Before Top 10 Checklist

1. **Three ethical theories**: Be able to apply each to the Trolley Problem and a computing scenario
2. **SE Code 8 principles**: Name them all; know 2-3 key clauses per principle
3. **Contextual Integrity**: Context, actors, appropriateness, distribution — apply to a data-sharing scenario
4. **Birsch's 5 criteria**: Causation, Knowledge, Intent, Capacity, Freedom — apply to Equifax
5. **Top 5 fallacies**: Ad hominem, straw man, false cause, bifurcation, hasty generalisation — recognise in text
6. **Fair use 4 factors**: Purpose, nature, amount, market effect
7. **Patent requirements**: Novel, non-obvious, useful
8. **GPL vs MIT**: Key difference = copyleft (derivatives must be open)
9. **Certification vs licensing**: Voluntary vs mandatory
10. **Problem of Many Hands**: Definition and how to address it using Birsch's criteria
