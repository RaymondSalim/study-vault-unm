## Recommended Study Order

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Introduction & Professional Ethics (L1) | Foundation: defines ethics, why it matters in computing, sets up the framework | 1.5 hours |
| 2 | Ethics Theories (L2) | Core toolkit: three frameworks (consequentialism, deontology, virtue) used throughout | 3 hours |
| 3 | Critical Thinking & Fallacies (L3) | Reasoning skills: how to construct and evaluate ethical arguments | 2.5 hours |
| 4 | Professionalism & Codes of Ethics (L4) | Applies theories: SE Code provides structure for analysing scenarios | 2.5 hours |
| 5 | Accountability & Responsibility (L9) | Deepens codes: Birsch's criteria give analytical framework for blame/responsibility | 2 hours |
| 6 | Privacy & Data Protection (L5) | Major topic: Contextual Integrity is the key privacy framework | 2.5 hours |
| 7 | Intellectual Property (L6) | Legal foundations: patents, copyright, fair use — practical knowledge | 2 hours |
| 8 | Open Source & Licensing (L10) | Extends IP: GPL/MIT/Apache as ethical models for software freedom | 1.5 hours |
| 9 | Safety & Reliability (L11) | Applied ethics: system failures and professional responsibility | 2 hours |
| 10 | Cyber-Behaviour (L8) | Applied ethics: cybercrime and professional conduct online | 1.5 hours |
| 11 | Research Ethics (L7) | Academic integrity: misconduct, authorship, human subjects | 2 hours |
| 12 | Autonomous & Pervasive Technology (L12) | Synthesis: combines all frameworks to analyse emerging technologies | 1.5 hours |

### Tips

- Topics 1–3 are foundations — master the three ethical theories and fallacies before moving on.
- Topic 4 (SE Code) is the analytical backbone — know the 8 principles and how to apply clauses to scenarios.
- Topic 5 (Accountability/Birsch) pairs directly with Topic 4 and system failure cases.
- Topic 6 (Privacy/CI) is highly examinable — learn Nissenbaum's framework thoroughly.
- Topics 7–8 (IP/Open Source) are intertwined — study together.
- Topic 11 (Research Ethics) connects to your coursework (ethical analysis assignment).
- Total estimated study time: ~24 hours (spread over 6–8 days recommended).
