## What is Intellectual Property?

Products of creativity and invention — different from tangible property.

The goal of IP law: ensure people with good ideas can profit from them, even after the idea is known.

## Four Types of IP Protection

| Type | Protects | Duration | Key Feature |
|------|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Patents** | Inventions (processes, machines) | ~20 years | Must be novel, non-obvious, useful |
| **Trademarks** | Signs distinguishing goods/services | 10 years (renewable) | Brand identity |
| **Copyright** | Original works of authorship | Life + 50-70 years | Automatic upon creation |
| **Trade Secrets** | Confidential business information | Unlimited (while secret) | Must be actively kept secret |

## Patents

### Requirements for Patentability
- **Novel**: Not previously invented
- **Non-obvious**: Solution not obvious to specialists
- **Useful**: Has practical application

### Key Characteristics
- Exclusive rights for limited period (~20 years)
- In exchange for **public disclosure** of the invention
- Third parties must pay a licence fee to use
- Territorial (must file in each country)

### Software and Patents
- Software itself is usually excluded from patents in many jurisdictions
- Components/methods may be patentable
- Controversial in computing (patent trolls, overly broad patents)

### Case: Amazon 1-Click Patent
- 1997: Amazon offered 1-Click purchasing
- 1999: Patent granted for the method
- 2000: Apple licensed the patent
- Controversial: Is "one-click purchasing" really a novel invention?

### Patent Trolls
- Entities that acquire patents solely to extract licensing fees through lawsuits
- Don't produce products themselves
- Burden of proof on defendant
- Fighting infringement is extremely expensive

## Trademarks

### Definition
A sign capable of distinguishing goods/services of one enterprise from others.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Trademark** | Distinguishes one enterprise's goods from others |
| **Collective mark** | Distinguishes goods of association members |
| **Certification mark** | Certifies origin, quality, accuracy |

### Functions
- **Origin**: Identifies source of products/services
- **Choice**: Helps consumers choose with ease
- **Quality**: Consumers associate with known quality
- **Marketing**: Promotes through advertising
- **Economic**: Valuable asset; can be licensed/franchised

### Registration (Malaysia)
- Exclusive right to use mark in trading
- Legal action for infringement
- Valid 10 years from application date (renewable)

## Copyright

### What it Covers
Original works of authorship in: literature, music, drama, graphic art, sculpture, motion pictures, sound recordings, architecture.

### Four Exclusive Rights
1. Right to **reproduce** the work
2. Right to prepare **derivative works**
3. Right to **distribute** copies
4. Right to **perform or display** publicly

### What Copyright Does NOT Cover
- Ideas, facts, or common knowledge
- Works not in tangible fixed form (e.g., unrecorded dance)

### Fair Use

Allows reproduction without permission for scholarship, teaching, research, criticism, commentary, news reporting.

**Four Factors** (all must be considered):
1. Purpose and character of use (commercial vs non-profit educational)
2. Nature of the copyrighted work
3. Amount and substantiality used relative to whole
4. Effect on potential market or value

### Fair Use Examples

| Scenario | Fair Use? | Reasoning |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Professor prints 2 chapters (35 pages each) for class | Debatable | Educational purpose, but substantial amount, market effect |
| Professor puts chapters on public web page | Likely NOT | Public access affects market |
| 10% from several books on password-protected site | More likely | Limited amount, restricted access, educational |

### Doctrine of First Sale
- Buy a physical copy → you own that copy
- Can resell, lend, or give away the physical object
- Copyright owner's control ends after first sale
- **Digital challenge**: "used" digital files are perfect copies — undermines creators' income

## Trade Secrets

- Confidential information with commercial value
- Can be sold or licensed
- Unauthorised acquisition/disclosure is unfair practice
- No registration needed — protected as long as kept secret

## IP Protection in Malaysia

| Law | Coverage |
|-----|----------|
| Patents Act 1983 | Inventions |
| Trade Marks Act 1976 | Brand identity |
| Copyright Act 1987 | Creative works |
| Industrial Designs Act 1996 | Product appearance |
| Layout-Designs of Integrated Circuits Act 2000 | Chip designs |
| Geographical Indications Act 2000 | Regional products |

Malaysia is a member of WIPO and signatory to Paris Convention, Berne Convention, and TRIPS (WTO).
