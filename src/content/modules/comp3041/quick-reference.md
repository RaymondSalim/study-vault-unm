## Ethical Theories Comparison

| Theory | Central Question | Judges By | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Consequentialism | What outcome maximises good? | Consequences/results | Pragmatic, results-oriented | Can justify harming innocents; hard to predict outcomes |
| Deontology | What are the rules/duties? | Whether rules are followed | Protects rights, clear rules | Rigid; rules can conflict |
| Virtue Ethics | What would a virtuous person do? | Character of the agent | Flexible, development-focused | Vague; virtues can differ culturally |

## Birsch's Criteria for Accountability

| Criterion | Question | More → Stronger accountability |
|-----------|----------|-------------------------------|
| Causation | Did they cause/contribute to the outcome? | Direct cause > indirect |
| Knowledge | Did they know the risks? | Knew > should have known > didn't know |
| Intent | Was it intentional? | Intentional > negligent > accidental |
| Capacity | Could they understand the consequences? | Expert > junior |
| Freedom | Did they act freely? | Free > pressured > coerced |

## Nissenbaum's Contextual Integrity

| Component | Key Question |
|-----------|-------------|
| Context | What sphere of life is this? (healthcare, education, commerce) |
| Actors | Who is the subject, sender, recipient? |
| Appropriateness | Is this type of info okay in this context? |
| Distribution | How should this info flow, and to whom? |
| Violation | Does the flow breach expected norms? |

## Intellectual Property Quick Reference

| Type | Protects | Duration | Registration |
|------|----------|----------|-------------|
| Patent | Inventions | ~20 years | Required |
| Copyright | Creative works | Life + 50-70 years | Automatic |
| Trademark | Brand identity | 10 years (renewable) | Required |
| Trade Secret | Confidential info | Unlimited | Not required |

## Fair Use — Four Factors

1. Purpose and character (commercial vs educational)
2. Nature of copyrighted work
3. Amount used relative to whole
4. Effect on market/value

## Common Fallacies (Top 10 for Exam)

| Fallacy | Quick Definition |
|---------|-----------------|
| Ad hominem | Attacking the person, not the argument |
| Straw man | Misrepresenting opponent's position |
| False cause | Correlation ≠ causation |
| Bifurcation | False binary (only two options presented) |
| Appeal to authority | Citing irrelevant expert |
| Hasty generalisation | Broad conclusion from small sample |
| Circular reasoning | Conclusion restates the premise |
| Cherry picking | Selecting only supporting evidence |
| Tu quoque | "You did it too" (hypocrisy charge) |
| Fallacy of composition | Parts true → whole true |
