## A

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Accountability** | Being answerable for what happened (backward-looking) |
| **ACM** | Association for Computing Machinery — professional computing organisation |
| **Ad hominem** | Fallacy: attacking the person rather than their argument |
| **Appropriateness (CI)** | Whether a type of information is okay to share in a given context |
| **Autonomous technology** | Systems that operate independently of human intervention |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Bifurcation** | Fallacy: presenting only two options when more exist (false binary) |
| **Birsch's criteria** | Framework for accountability: causation, knowledge, intent, capacity, freedom |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Categorical Imperative** | Kant's rule: act only by maxims you could will as universal law |
| **Certification** | Voluntary recognition of professional competence by non-governmental body |
| **Cherry picking** | Fallacy: selecting only evidence that supports your argument |
| **Circular reasoning** | Fallacy: conclusion restates the premise |
| **Consequentialism** | Ethical theory judging actions by their outcomes/consequences |
| **Context collapse** | When distinct social contexts blur, destroying the norms governing each |
| **Contextual Integrity** | Nissenbaum's privacy framework: privacy = appropriate information flow per context norms |
| **Copyright** | IP protection for original works of authorship |
| **Copyleft** | Licensing ensuring derivative works remain open source (e.g., GPL) |
| **Cultural relativism** | Theory that morality depends on society's guidelines; no universal morality |
| **Cyber-specific crime** | Crime that can only be committed through cyber-technology |

## D

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Deontology** | Ethical theory judging actions by whether they follow rules/duties |
| **Dependability** | Ability of a system to deliver service that can justifiably be trusted |
| **Differential privacy** | Adding statistical noise to data queries to protect individual identity |
| **Distribution (CI)** | Norms governing how information should flow and to whom |
| **Divine Command Theory** | Morality determined by God's commands |
| **Doctrine of First Sale** | Copyright owner's control ends after first sale of physical copy |

## E

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Ethics** | Branch of philosophy addressing questions of morality (right/wrong, good/bad, just/unjust) |
| **Ethics of Caring** | Theory: morality through maintaining caring relationships |
| **Ethics of Justice** | Rawls: justice as fairness; social contract must protect least-advantaged |

## F

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Fabrication** | Research misconduct: making up data or results |
| **Fair use** | Right to reproduce copyrighted material without permission for scholarship, criticism, etc. |
| **Fallacy** | Defect in an argument where conclusion is not supported by propositions |
| **Falsification** | Research misconduct: manipulating data to misrepresent research |
| **Federated learning** | Training algorithms across decentralised devices without centralising raw data |

## G

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **GDPR** | General Data Protection Regulation (EU, 2018) — comprehensive data protection law |
| **GPL** | GNU General Public License — copyleft licence requiring derivatives remain open source |

## I

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **IEEE** | Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers — professional engineering body |
| **Informed consent** | Subject's voluntary agreement based on understanding of research/data use |
| **Intellectual property** | Products of creativity and invention (patents, copyright, trademarks, trade secrets) |

## K

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Kultgen's Wheel** | Framework of attributes that define a profession |

## L

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Liability** | Legal obligation to compensate for harm caused |
| **Licensing** | Mandatory government permission to practice a profession |

## M

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **MNCC** | Malaysian National Computer Confederation — professional computing body in Malaysia |

## N

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Natural Law Theory** | Morality based on human nature; moral obligations from promoting natural good |
| **Nissenbaum, Helen** | Scholar who developed the Contextual Integrity framework for privacy |
| **Nuremberg Code** | First guidelines for ethical use of human research subjects (1947) |

## P

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Patent** | Exclusive right granted for an invention (~20 years) in exchange for public disclosure |
| **PDPA** | Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act 2010 |
| **Pervasive technology** | Technology that has spread widely, becoming ubiquitous and embedded in daily life |
| **Plagiarism** | Appropriating another's ideas/work without giving credit |
| **Problem of Many Hands** | Difficulty of assigning accountability when responsibility is distributed |
| **Professional ethics** | Ethical principles specific to a particular profession |
| **Profiling** | Constructing representation of individual by analysing their data |

## R

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Responsibility** | Duty to ensure good outcomes (forward-looking) |

## S

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **SE Code** | Software Engineering Code of Ethics and Professional Practice (ACM/IEEE) |
| **Straw man** | Fallacy: misrepresenting opponent's argument to make it easier to attack |

## T

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Trade secret** | Confidential information with commercial value, protected while kept secret |
| **Trademark** | Sign distinguishing goods/services of one enterprise from others |
| **Tu quoque** | Fallacy: dismissing argument by charging hypocrisy ("you too") |

## U

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Utilitarianism** | Branch of consequentialism: "greatest good for the greatest number" |

## V

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Virtue ethics** | Ethical theory focused on developing virtuous character (Aristotle) |
