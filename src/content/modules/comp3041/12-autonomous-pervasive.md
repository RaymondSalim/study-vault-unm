## Key Definitions

| Concept | Definition | Key Feature |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Pervasive Technology** | Technology that has spread widely throughout society | Ubiquitous, often invisible, embedded in daily life |
| **Autonomous Technology** | Systems that operate independently of human intervention | Decision-making freedom without outside constraints |

### Important Distinctions

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| Autonomy = Intelligence | Autonomous systems don't need AI |
| Autonomy = Robot | Not all autonomous tech has a physical body |
| Robot = Autonomous + AI | These are three separate concepts |

### Clarifying Three Concepts

| Concept | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Robot** | Electro-mechanical device capable of movement/action | Manufacturing arm, vacuum robot |
| **Artificial Intelligence** | Human-made artifact with ability to learn and reason | ChatGPT, image recognition |
| **Autonomous Technology** | Can operate without human intervention | Thermostat, autopilot |

## The Autonomy–Pervasiveness Cycle

### Path 1: Autonomy → Pervasiveness
- Greater autonomy → cheaper to use
- Fewer human operators → lower costs → more affordable → wider adoption
- **Example**: Chatbots replaced expensive call centres, making 24/7 support affordable for small businesses

### Path 2: Pervasiveness → Autonomy
- Large user base → more revenue potential
- Justify automation investment → increased autonomy
- **Example**: Once everyone had smartphones, companies invested in AI assistants (Siri, Google Assistant)

## Convergence in Action

| Domain | How Autonomy + Pervasiveness Combine |
|--------|--------------------------------------|
| Autonomous Vehicles + IoT | Self-driving cars communicate with smart traffic lights; sensors optimise routes |
| Smart Homes | Autonomous temperature/lighting + pervasive monitoring across all rooms |
| Social Media Algorithms | Autonomous content curation + pervasive presence (billions of users); self-reinforcing |
| Predictive Maintenance | Autonomous diagnosis + pervasive data collection from all connected devices |

## Ethical Issues

### Case: Pervasiveness of Camera Phones
- Camera Phone Predator Alert Act (2009, proposed)
- Every person may carry 7+ cameras daily
- Changed relationship with photography and privacy
- **Lifelogging**: Recording every moment (audio + video, never turned off)
  - Benefits: Memory aid, accountability, evidence
  - Dangers: Constant surveillance, privacy of others, data security

### Case: Injured by GPS (2010)
- Lauren Rosenberg followed Google Maps walking directions onto a highway with no sidewalks
- Hit by car; sued Google for negligent directions
- **Question**: Who is responsible when human + computer collaborate on a decision with bad outcome?

| Party | Argument for Responsibility |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Google | Provided directions without adequate safety warnings |
| The user | Should have used common sense; saw it was a highway |
| The driver | Hit a pedestrian (negligent driving) |

### Key Ethical Questions

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Who is responsible when autonomous systems cause harm? | Gap between traditional liability and autonomous action |
| Should autonomous systems make life/death decisions? | Self-driving car dilemmas |
| What happens when pervasive tech creates surveillance? | Power imbalance between collectors and subjects |
| How do we maintain human agency? | Dependence on autonomous systems may erode autonomy |
| Who controls the algorithms? | Opacity of decision-making in pervasive systems |

## Ethical Framework Application

### Self-Driving Buses on Campus (Discussion Scenario)

| Stakeholder | Concern | Framework |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| Students | Personal safety, reliability | **Consequentialism**: Outcomes for users |
| Staff | Job displacement, trust | **Virtue Ethics**: Responsible innovation |
| Administrators | Liability, cost-benefit | **Deontology**: Duty of care |
| Safety advocates | Fail-safe design, testing | **Professional codes**: Ensure adequate testing |

### Questions to Ask About Any Autonomous/Pervasive System:

1. **Reliability**: How does it guarantee consistent operation without unexpected errors?
2. **Safety**: How does it minimise risk to people who use or encounter it?
3. **Accountability**: Who is responsible when something goes wrong?
4. **Transparency**: Can users understand how decisions are made?
5. **Consent**: Have affected people agreed to interact with this system?
