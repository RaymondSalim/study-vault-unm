## What is a Profession?

> "A disciplined group of individuals who adhere to high ethical standards and uphold themselves to, and are accepted by, the public as possessing special knowledge and skills in a widely recognised, organised body of learning derived from education and training at a high level, and who are prepared to exercise this knowledge and these skills in the interest of others." — Australian Council of Professions

### Kultgen's Attributes of Professions

A fully developed profession has:
- Specialised knowledge and training
- Code of ethics
- Certification/licensing process
- Professional associations
- Service orientation (public interest)

## Path to Entering a Profession

```
Education → Accreditation → Certification → Licensing → Professional Society → Code of Ethics → Continuing Development
```

### Certification vs Licensing

| Aspect | Certification | Licensing |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Nature | Voluntary | Non-voluntary (mandatory) |
| Granted by | Non-governmental professional organisation | Government agency |
| Purpose | Attests mastery of specific knowledge/skills | Grants permission to practice |
| Consequence | Recognition of competence | Illegal to practice without one |

## Licensed Software Engineers

Required when working on systems whose failure could affect the public.

Requirements: Degree + Engineering exam + Work experience + Good moral character + Continuing education + Principles and practice exam

## The SE Code of Ethics and Professional Practice

### Preamble
- Software engineers have opportunities to do good or harm
- Should be committed to doing good
- Eight principles identify key ethical relationships
- Code should be seen as a **whole**, not a collection of parts
- Public interest is **paramount**

### Eight Principles

| # | Principle | Focus |
|---|-----------|-------|
| 1 | **Public Interest** | Act consistently with the public interest |
| 2 | **Client & Employer** | Act in best interests of client/employer, consistent with public interest |
| 3 | **Product** | Ensure product meets highest professional standards |
| 4 | **Judgment** | Maintain integrity and independence in professional judgment |
| 5 | **Management** | Promote ethical approach to software management |
| 6 | **Profession** | Advance integrity and reputation of the profession |
| 7 | **Colleagues** | Be fair to and supportive of colleagues |
| 8 | **Self** | Participate in lifelong learning, promote ethical practice |

### Key Clauses (Selected)

| Clause | Content |
|--------|---------|
| 1.01 | Accept full responsibility for own work |
| 1.03 | Approve software only if it is safe |
| 1.04 | Disclose actual/potential dangers |
| 2.05 | Respect confidentiality |
| 2.09 | Put employer's/client's interests first unless overriding moral concern |
| 3.10 | Ensure adequate testing, debugging, and review |
| 4.05 | Disclose conflicts of interest |
| 5.12 | Do not punish anyone for expressing ethical concerns |
| 6.13 | "Blow the whistle" when no alternative to reporting significant violations |
| 8.07 | Do not treat others unfairly because of prejudices |

## Alternative Fundamental Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Be impartial | Public good = company good = personal good |
| Disclose information | Don't let others come to harm by concealing information |
| Respect rights of others | Privacy, property, intellectual property |
| Treat others justly | Fair wages, appropriate credit, no discrimination |
| Take responsibility | For actions AND inactions |
| Share knowledge | Volunteer skills, help bring others up |
| Maintain integrity | Deliver commitments, obey law |
| Continually improve | Stay current, improve skills |
| Supervise responsibly | Set up training, monitor quality, maintain communication |

## Applying the Code: Worked Example

### Scenario: Dan finds illegal material on an employee's computer

**Step 1**: Identify relevant principles → Be impartial, Respect rights, Treat justly, Maintain integrity

**Step 2**: Examine relevant clauses:
- 2.03: Employee misused company equipment → obligation to report
- 2.09: Revealing images may harm employee (illegal possession)
- 3.13: Dan discovered material by violating company privacy policy
- 5.10: Files on computer ≠ guilt (could be stored by someone else)

**Conclusion**: Dan was wrong to violate policy, but once he has the knowledge, he is obliged to share it with company authorities — discreetly.

## MNCC Code of Ethics (Malaysia)

Six principles:
1. Act with **integrity** and competence
2. Act with **discretion** regarding confidential information
3. Act with **impartiality** when giving advice; disclose conflicts of interest
4. Accept full **responsibility** for work undertaken
5. Continuously enhance **professional knowledge**
6. Conduct self properly; not seek personal advantage to detriment of MNCC
