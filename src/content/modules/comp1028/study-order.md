## Recommended Study Sequence

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Java Basics (variables, types, control flow) | Foundation for everything; you cannot write any code without this | 3 hours |
| 2 | Arrays and Methods | Core data structure and code organisation; used in all subsequent topics | 4 hours |
| 3 | OOP Basics (classes, objects, encapsulation) | Introduces the paradigm used throughout the rest of the module | 4 hours |
| 4 | Inheritance and Polymorphism | Builds directly on classes; most heavily tested OOP concept | 5 hours |
| 5 | Interfaces and Collections | Extends OOP with abstraction; ArrayList/HashMap used in algorithm implementations | 3 hours |
| 6 | Recursion | Requires solid method understanding; conceptually challenging and needs practice | 5 hours |
| 7 | Searching and Sorting | Applies everything (arrays, methods, recursion for merge sort); natural culmination | 4 hours |
| 8 | Exceptions and I/O | Relatively self-contained; useful but less conceptually dependent on other topics | 2 hours |

## Study Tips

- **Basics first** — do not skip ahead; every topic builds on the previous one.
- **OOP is the core** — inheritance and polymorphism questions dominate exams; make sure you can trace dynamic dispatch.
- **Recursion needs practice** — solve at least 10 different recursive problems (factorial, Fibonacci, list processing, tree traversal).
- **Sorting algorithms** — be able to trace through each step by hand, not just describe them.
- **Code by hand** — practise writing code on paper, as exams are handwritten; pay attention to syntax details.

## Total Estimated Time: ~30 hours

Allocate extra practice time for recursion and sorting — these are the topics students struggle with most.
