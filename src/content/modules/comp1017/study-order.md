## Recommended Study Sequence

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Sets and Functions | Foundation for all other topics; notation used everywhere | 4 hours |
| 2 | Relations | Builds directly on sets; needed for understanding order and equivalence | 3 hours |
| 3 | Logic (Propositional and Predicate) | Required for writing proofs and formal reasoning in all subsequent topics | 4 hours |
| 4 | Proof Techniques | Central skill for the module; induction, contradiction, and direct proof appear in every remaining topic | 5 hours |
| 5 | Number Theory | Applies proof techniques immediately; GCD and modular arithmetic are self-contained | 4 hours |
| 6 | Sequences and Series | Uses induction heavily; algebraic manipulation practice before calculus | 4 hours |
| 7 | Calculus (Differentiation and Integration) | Relies on algebraic fluency from previous topics; often the largest exam section | 5 hours |

## Study Tips

- **Sets/Functions first** because every other topic references set notation and function terminology.
- **Logic before Proofs** because you need to understand logical structure before constructing arguments.
- **Number Theory after Proofs** because it is the most natural application of induction and direct proof.
- **Calculus last** because it is the most mechanically intensive and benefits from algebraic fluency built in earlier topics.

## Total Estimated Time: ~29 hours

Plan for 4-5 study sessions per topic, each 45-60 minutes with breaks.
