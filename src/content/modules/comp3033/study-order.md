## Recommended Study Order

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Introduction & Quality Definitions (L1) | Foundation: defines what quality means and the two QA strategies | 1.5 hours |
| 2 | Quality Models & NFRs (L2) | Builds quality vocabulary: McCall, ISO 25010, writing testable NFRs | 2.5 hours |
| 3 | Testing Fundamentals (L3) | Core concepts: V&V, Fault-Error-Failure, testing levels and limitations | 2 hours |
| 4 | Test Design & Coverage (L4) | Technical heart: partitioning, BVA, CFGs, cyclomatic complexity | 3 hours |
| 5 | Test Automation & JUnit (L5) | Applies test design: RIPR model, JUnit syntax, parameterized tests | 2.5 hours |
| 6 | Agile & TDD (L6) | Process context: why TDD, user stories, CI principles, Espresso | 2 hours |
| 7 | Software Metrics (L7) | Quantitative quality: LOC, complexity, C&K OO metrics | 2 hours |
| 8 | SQA Management (L8) | Big picture: org vs project level, SQA plans, integrating testing | 2 hours |
| 9 | Security & Performance Testing (L9-10) | Applied testing: SAST/DAST/SCA, OWASP, load/stress/soak | 2.5 hours |
| 10 | Exam Prep & Synthesis (L11) | Practice worked examples, identify weak areas | 2 hours |

### Tips

- Topics 1–3 are foundations — understand these before moving to technical test design.
- Topic 4 is the most calculation-heavy — practice drawing CFGs and computing cyclomatic complexity by hand.
- Topics 5–6 are intertwined — JUnit implements what test design specifies; TDD ties them together.
- Topic 7 (metrics) can be studied in any order but connects back to cyclomatic complexity from Topic 4.
- Topic 9 (security/performance) is standalone but references NFRs from Topic 2.
- Total estimated study time: ~22 hours (spread over 5–7 days recommended).
