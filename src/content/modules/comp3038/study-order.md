## Recommended Study Order

| # | Topic | Why this order | Estimated time |
|---|-------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Supervised Basics (Linear/Logistic Regression) | Foundation for all ML -- introduces loss functions, gradient descent, overfitting | 3 hours |
| 2 | Classification (kNN, Naive Bayes) | Simple classifiers that introduce distance metrics and probabilistic reasoning | 2 hours |
| 3 | Decision Trees and Ensembles | Builds on classification; introduces tree-based reasoning, then bagging/boosting | 3 hours |
| 4 | SVMs | Requires understanding of classification and optimisation from earlier topics | 3 hours |
| 5 | Neural Networks | Uses gradient descent from topic 1; introduces backpropagation and network architectures | 3 hours |
| 6 | Deep Learning | Extends neural networks to CNNs and modern architectures | 2.5 hours |
| 7 | Unsupervised Learning (k-means, PCA, clustering) | Independent of supervised topics but benefits from understanding of distance/similarity | 2.5 hours |
| 8 | Evaluation and Methodology | Applies to all previous topics; best studied last so you can evaluate all models | 2 hours |

### Tips

- Topics 1-2 are the entry point -- if you understand loss minimisation and gradient descent, everything else follows.
- Topics 3-4 are the "classical ML" core -- often heavily examined with hand calculations.
- Topics 5-6 are the "deep learning" block -- ensure you can do backprop by hand for a small network.
- Topic 7 stands alone -- good for a change of pace between study sessions.
- Topic 8 is cross-cutting -- review it last and apply to all other topics.
- Total estimated study time: ~21 hours (spread over 5-6 days recommended).
